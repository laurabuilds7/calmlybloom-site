What this is (and isn't)

Not another crypto pitch.

No signals. No tips. No "just trust me" tables of green numbers. No guarantees of any returns, ever. This isn't a trading service and I'm not a financial advisor.

What I am is someone who walked into crypto as a complete outsider, asked a lot of dumb questions, made a lot of small mistakes, and slowly built a relationship with it that feels manageable. Not exciting. Not dramatic. Manageable. That's the part nobody seems to talk about.

If you're curious about crypto but every resource you've opened so far has felt like it was written by a 22-year-old shouting from a Lamborghini, this is the opposite of that.

The free call

What a 30-minute conversation looks like.

The call is free, no pressure, and exists for one reason: so you can work out if this is for you. Here's what happens, start to finish.

  1. You tell me where you're at. What's got you curious. What's making you hesitant. What you've already tried (if anything).
  2. I ask more questions than I answer. The first 20 minutes are about understanding your situation — your goals, your comfort with risk, your timeline, what "financial independence" actually looks like for you.
  3. I show you one real example. Something concrete — how a specific decision might play out, with real numbers, in a way you can follow.
  4. You decide what's next. Maybe that's joining a community I work with. Maybe it's a resource list and us parting ways. Maybe it's "let's talk again in a month." No sales close, no countdown offer.

Who this is for

The honest filter.

You'll probably get something out of this if…

You're curious about crypto but the usual rooms feel loud, gendered, or hostile to a real question.

You want financial options — not a side hustle, not a grind — and you're willing to learn something slowly.

You've been "going to look into this" for a year and want a warm, judgement-free starting point.

This probably isn't for you if…

You're looking for "what coin should I buy this week?" — I won't tell you, and neither should anyone else.

You want guaranteed returns or overnight results. Crypto isn't that, and anyone who says otherwise is selling you something.

You already trade actively and are looking for a signals service. That's a different kind of product.

A bit about me

Laura, from Cape Town.

Frequently, gently asked

Questions people actually ask on the phone.

Is this a scam?

Honest answer: I'd be suspicious too. The free call is 30 minutes, on video, with a real person (me). I don't ask for money, card details, or any financial information on the call. If at the end of it I think there's a community or resource that would suit you, I'll mention it — and that mention is the only thing you'd ever pay me for, and only if you opted in afterwards. If nothing fits, we part ways and you've lost 30 minutes.

Are you a licensed financial advisor?

No. I'm explicitly not a licensed financial services provider and I don't give regulated financial advice. What I share is personal experience and educational context. Any actual financial decision you make is yours; for regulated advice, speak to a licensed FSP registered with the South African FSCA.

How much money do I need to have to talk to you?

None, for the call. The call is free. What you choose to do after the call is entirely up to you, and there are paths in that don't require a large starting amount.

I've never used crypto. Is this too early?

It's actually the better time. The conversation is easier when you don't have habits or assumptions to unwind. Come with your questions and nothing else.

What does "managing risk" actually mean here?

It means deciding in advance how much you're willing to lose, how you'll know when something's not working, and what part of your life crypto is and isn't allowed to touch. It's boring on purpose. We'll talk about it on the call.

Is this only for women?

The tone and the people I've mostly worked with skew female, but the conversation is open to anyone who resonates with the "calm, not complicated" framing.
