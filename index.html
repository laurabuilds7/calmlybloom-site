A two-minute note from me — why this exists, and who it's for.

Let's be honest

Crypto feels scary. And honestly? That's completely valid.

I am not going to pretend the reasons people stay out of this world aren't real. They are. Three walls I see people hit, and what we'll talk through if you book a chat.

01

The complexity is overwhelming.

Blockchains, wallets, gas fees, seed phrases. Most people feel lost before they've even started, and that's not a failing — it's a vocabulary that was never built with you in mind.

02

The volatility is genuinely frightening.

Prices swing wildly, sometimes overnight. Without a calm framing and someone who's sat through it before, it can feel like a rollercoaster with no seatbelt.

03

The scams have made trust nearly impossible.

So many promises, so many vanished funds. The mistrust isn't paranoia — it's a rational response to a space that has badly let people down.

You don't need to become a crypto expert. You just need a calm starting point and someone honest to think out loud with.

What this is, and isn't

Not another crypto pitch.

No signals. No tips. No "just trust me" tables of green numbers. No promises or guarantees of any returns of any kind, ever. This is not a trading service. It is not investment advice. I am not a licensed financial advisor and I am not a registered Financial Services Provider.

What I am is someone who walked into crypto as a complete outsider, asked a lot of beginner questions, made a lot of small mistakes, and slowly built a relationship with it that feels manageable. Not exciting. Not dramatic. Manageable. That is the part nobody seems to talk about.

If you are curious about crypto but every resource you have opened so far felt like it was written by a 22-year-old shouting from a Lamborghini, this is the opposite of that.

The free chat

What a 30-minute conversation looks like.

The chat is free, it's on Zoom or WhatsApp, no slides, no pitch deck. Here is what happens, start to finish.

  1. You tell me where you're at.

    What's got you curious. What's making you hesitant. What you've already tried, if anything.

  2. I ask more questions than I answer.

    The first part is about understanding your situation — your goals, your comfort with uncertainty, your timeline, what financial freedom actually looks like for you. Not a generic version of it. Yours.

  3. I share what learning looks like, in plain language.

    Conceptual, never prescriptive. The point is to give you a feel for how I think about all of this — not to tell you what to do with your money. Specific decisions about specific assets are not part of this conversation.

  4. You decide what's next.

    Maybe that's an educational community I think you'd find calming and clear. Maybe it's a short reading list and us parting ways. Maybe it's "let's talk again in a month." No sales close, no countdown timer, no upsell at the door.

Who this is for

The honest filter.

You'll probably get something out of this if…

You're curious about crypto but the usual rooms feel loud, gendered, or hostile to a real beginner question.

You want options for your future — not a side hustle, not a grind — and you're willing to learn something slowly.

You've been "going to look into this" for a year and want a warm, judgement-free starting point.

This probably isn't for you if…

You're looking for "what coin should I buy this week?" — I won't tell you, and neither should anyone else who's being honest.

You want guaranteed returns or overnight results. Crypto is not that, and anyone who says otherwise is selling you something.

You already trade actively and are looking for a signals service. That's a different kind of product, and I am not it.

A bit about me

Laura, mom first.

I didn't come from tech. I didn't come from finance. I had exactly zero interest in being the loudest person in any room. Crypto happened to me slowly — one honest conversation at a time, with people who were patient with my questions instead of impressed by their own answers.

What I noticed, and what still bothers me, is that most of the door into this world has been guarded by men in expensive cars and 19-year-olds yelling about "cycles." There is a quieter, more practical version of this story. It involves patience, slow learning, being honest with yourself about what you can afford to lose, and the freedom to build something on your own terms — not as a side grind, but as part of a softer, more unhurried life.

My goal on the chat isn't to convert you. It's to give you the conversation I wish I'd had on day one — the one that would have saved me a year of wondering whether this was for me.

Questions people actually ask on the phone.

Is this a scam?

Honest answer: I would be suspicious too. The free chat is 30 minutes, on video, with a real person — me. I don't ask for money, card details, or any financial information on the chat. If at the end I think there's an educational community or resource you might enjoy, I'll mention it. Whether you ever do anything with that suggestion is entirely your call. Are you a licensed financial advisor?

No. I am explicitly not a licensed Financial Services Provider, I am not a registered financial advisor, and I do not give regulated financial advice. Anything I share — on a chat, on this site, anywhere — is personal experience and general educational context. Every financial decision you make is yours alone. For any regulated advice, speak to a licensed FSP registered with the South African FSCA (or the equivalent regulator in your country).

How much money do I need to have to talk to you?

None, for the chat. The chat is free. What you choose to do afterwards is entirely up to you, and the chat itself does not require, request, or involve any financial commitment.

I've never used crypto. Is this too early?

It is actually a better time. The conversation is easier when you don't have habits or assumptions to unwind. Come with your questions and nothing else.

I learn alongside a small group of people who have been around crypto longer than I have. The chat is just with me. No. Specific investment decisions about specific assets are not part of this conversation, ever. If you want that kind of advice, please speak with a licensed Financial Services Provider in your jurisdiction.
